Keyman Design and Devlopment

I was contracted to do a complete overhaul of the website's front-end. Assess issues with the old UX design, and develop a UI that gives the website a more updated look and feel.

Challenge

The website is unfamiliar, complex and outdated (i.e. no one has updated the site’s UI or navigation since its creation in 2005); additionally, the original developer is no longer under their employment. According to the client, a lot of the most important information was not easily accessible. There were also a lot of dead-ends and irrelevant or outdated information throughout the site.

Solution and Implementation

While reviewing each page, I simultaneously created a site map. This allowed me to streamline my review process, gain an in-depth understanding of the product and create the documentation I would later need to pinpoint usability issues to those unfamiliar with the site’s complexity. I then interviewed the client on what his goals were for the website to gain an understanding of what they valued as important in the site.
Using the site-map as a guide alongside the information gained from interviewing the client, I was able to mock-up a new design which emphasized the most important information and gave the website a more clean and updated feel. The mock-up allowed the client to get a clear understanding of the new design before starting development. After approving the mock-up with the client I coded the HTML, CSS, and some of the JS of the design with the collaboration of a back-end developer to finish the project.
